# TOGAF® Exam Preparation - Important Terms Glossary

This glossary contains key terms and definitions extracted directly from the target exam slides. Knowing these definitions precisely is critical for scoring well on the TOGAF® Foundation exam.

---

### A
* **ADM (Architecture Development Method):** The core process of the TOGAF Standard. It is a tested, repeatable, and non-waterfall iterative cycle consisting of 10 phases designed to plan, develop, implement, govern, change, and sustain an Enterprise Architecture.
* **Application Architecture:** The BDAT domain that provides a blueprint for individual applications to be deployed, their interactions, and their relationships to the core business processes of the organization.
* **Approved Deliverable:** A document that has undergone formal review and approval. While approved, it is **not** necessarily finalized and can still evolve during subsequent ADM phases under strict change control.
* **Architecture Definition Document:** The *qualitative* view of the architecture design, serving as a companion to the quantitative Architecture Requirements Specification.
* **Architecture Principles:** Underlying general rules and guidelines for the use of resources and assets, forming the basis for making architecture decisions. They are categorized into Business, Data, Application, and Technology principles.
* **Architecture Requirements Specification:** The *quantitative* view of the solution, stating the criteria and compliance requirements that an implementation project must meet.
* **Architecture View:** A specific representation of a system from the perspective of a related set of concerns, composed of one or more models, and created to be meaningful to stakeholders.
* **Architecture Viewpoint:** The vantage point or perspective that determines what can be seen of a system. It defines the conventions, rules, and schemas used to construct, interpret, and use a view to address specific concerns. Reusable and stored in libraries.

### B
* **BDAT Domains:** The four subsets of Enterprise Architecture supported by TOGAF: Business, Data, Application, and Technology.
* **Building Block (BB):** A package of functionality defined to meet business needs across an organization, recognizable by domain experts. It is reusable, replaceable, has defined boundaries, and is loosely coupled to its implementation.
* **Business Architecture:** The BDAT domain that defines the business strategy, governance, organization, and key business processes.
* **Business Principles, Goals, & Drivers:** Deliverables that define the culture, strategy, and direction of the organization, providing the business context that guides and constrains the EA capability.

### C
* **Capability Architecture:** The landscape level that supports the development of roadmaps aimed at realizing specific capability increments.
* **Communications Plan:** A deliverable that defines how, when, and to whom architecture information is communicated to ensure effective stakeholder engagement.
* **Conceptual Abstraction:** The abstraction level focused on requirements and service models (understanding the business, application, or technology service needs).
* **Concern:** An interest in a system relevant to one or more of its stakeholders and their goals (e.g., performance, reliability, security, availability, and cost).
* **Contextual Abstraction:** The abstraction level focused on understanding the environment, scope, motivations, goals, and objectives of the architecture work.

### D
* **Data Architecture:** The BDAT domain describing the structure of an organization's conceptual, logical, and physical data assets and data management resources.
* **Draft Deliverable:** A document under development that has not yet undergone formal review or approval.

### E
* **Enterprise:** Any collection of organizations that have common goals. It can comprise partners, suppliers, customer networks, or multiple business units, and is considered as a system.
* **Enterprise Scope (Breadth):** A dimension of scoping that defines the horizontal extent of the enterprise covered by the architecture effort (e.g., organizations, business units, departments, or processes).

### L
* **Level of Detail (Depth):** A dimension of scoping that defines how deep the architecture effort goes and establishes the boundary between architecture and detailed system design/development.
* **Logical Abstraction:** The abstraction level focused on identifying implementation-independent components (logical business, data, application, or technology components).

### P
* **Physical Abstraction:** The abstraction level focused on evaluating physical component alternatives for allocation and implementation.
* **Planning Horizon (Time Period):** A dimension of scoping that defines the time period articulated for the Architecture Vision and detailed Architecture Description.

### R
* **Request for Architecture Work:** An official high-level document sent from the sponsoring organization to the architecture organization to trigger the start of an ADM cycle.

### S
* **Segment Architecture:** The landscape level that supports direction setting and roadmap development at a program or portfolio level.
* **SMART Outcomes:** The criteria for business scenario outcomes: **S**pecific, **M**easurable, **A**ctionable, **R**ealistic, and **T**ime-bound.
* **SPIN Technique:** A sales technique (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff) used to gather business requirement information that can inform a Business Scenario.
* **Stakeholder:** An individual, team, organization, or class thereof having an interest in a system.
* **Statement of Architecture Work:** An approved document that defines the scope, plans, program of works, and governance constraints to deploy the architecture outlined in the Architecture Vision.
* **Strategic Architecture:** The landscape level that supports direction-setting at the executive level.

### T
* **Technology Architecture:** The BDAT domain describing the digital architecture, software, and hardware infrastructure capabilities and standards required to support deployment of services.
* **TOGAF Library:** A portfolio of additional guidance material (templates, patterns, reference material) maintained by The Open Group Architecture Forum to support the application of the TOGAF Standard.
* **TOGAF Standard:** A best-practice framework for Enterprise Architecture, providing a standard cycle of change (ADM) and structural descriptions of building blocks.
