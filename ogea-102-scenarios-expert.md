# OGEA-102 TOGAF EA Practitioner — 100 Expert Scenario Questions

**Source:** csahli/TOGAF_EA_10_Prep, xde013/togaf, Gana-Dube/togaf-exam-hub, ITExams/ExamTopics OG0-092  
**Difficulty:** Practitioner — 5-point scoring, multi-option ranking, full realistic scenarios

---

## Format

Each question presents a detailed business scenario followed by 4 response options. The options are scored:
- **5 pts** = Best answer (full marks)
- **3 pts** = Second-best (partial credit)
- **1 pt** = Third-best (minimal credit)
- **0 pts** = Distractor (no credit)

---

### Q1 — Granite Bank: Technology Architecture Standards

**Scenario:** Granite Bank is under pressure from its CIO to reduce cost and operational risk from inconsistent technologies across autonomous delivery teams. The CIO also insists the bank must adopt promising new technologies quickly as innovation is a competitive differentiator. Phases A–C are complete. Phase D is developing the Technology Architecture. Some delivery teams fear standardization will forbid new technology; infrastructure leaders fear unrestricted choice will keep costs high.

**Question:** What is the most appropriate way for the architect to proceed?

- A. Mandate a single standard technology stack for all teams with no exceptions
- B. Publish a recommended technology standards catalogue but make it advisory
- C. Define a Target Technology Architecture with a standards catalogue; perform gap analysis; establish a governed dispensation process for justified non-standard adoption
- D. Allow every team to select any technology with no standards

**Answers:** A=3, B=1, **C=5**, D=0

## Scoring Guide

For the 5-point scored questions (Q1–20, Q31–40), the best answer scores 5 points. In the actual OGEA-102 exam, you choose the **single best answer** — but understanding why the other options are second-best or distractors is key to passing.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **5** | Best — directly solves the problem, fully aligned with TOGAF |
| **3** | Second-best — partially correct but misses key elements |
| **1** | Third-best — minimal alignment, weak approach |
| **0** | Distractor — wrong, anti-pattern, or harmful |
