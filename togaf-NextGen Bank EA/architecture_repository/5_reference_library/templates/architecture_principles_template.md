## 2. Principle Formulation Methodology

In compliance with the TOGAF Standard, each principle must contain the following components:
1.  **Name**: A clear, memorable title that conveys the core idea. Avoid generic naming.
2.  **Statement**: A unambiguous directive outlining the core rule.
3.  **Rationale**: The business benefit or underlying reason for implementing this principle.
4.  **Implications**: The tangible requirements, costs, or restrictions imposed on the organization, processes, or technologies to satisfy the principle.

## 7. Governance, Waivers & Compliance

*   **Principle Adherence**: All newly proposed designs must map back to these principles during the Phase A/B/C/D stage-gates.
*   **Waiver Process**: If a project team cannot comply with a specific principle, they must file a formal **Architecture Waiver Request** outlining the business justification and remediation plan.
*   **Review Cycle**: These principles are reviewed annually by the **Architecture Board** to ensure alignment with shifting business strategies.
